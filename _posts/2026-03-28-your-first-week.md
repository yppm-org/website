This is your full move-in week checklist. Follow it in order so nothing gets missed.

## 7 DAYS BEFORE MOVE-IN

- Confirm your **move-in date and time** with us
- Make sure **electric and gas are active in your name** starting that day
- Schedule **internet installation**
- Set up **mail forwarding with USPS**
- Confirm your **renter’s insurance start date matches move-in**
- Start packing essentials separately (things you need right away)

## 3 DAYS BEFORE MOVE-IN

- Re-check that **utilities are confirmed and active**
- Confirm **payment method is working correctly**
- Gather:
  - ID
  - lease copy
  - insurance confirmation

- Pack a “first night” bag:
  - toiletries
  - chargers
  - basic tools
  - paper towels and trash bags

## MOVE-IN DAY

- Attend the **walkthrough and condition check**

- Go slowly and write down everything you see

- Take **photos of every room, wall, and appliance**

- Test basic systems:
  - lights
  - outlets
  - faucets
  - toilet flush
  - appliances

- Locate:
  - circuit breaker panel
  - water shut-off (if accessible)
  - trash and recycling area

- Receive keys and confirm locks work

## WITHIN 24–48 HOURS AFTER MOVE-IN

This is one of the most important steps.

- Submit your **full condition notes in writing**
- Include anything you noticed:
  - damage
  - wear
  - things not working properly

This protects you later. If it is not reported now, it may be treated as your responsibility

## DAY 2–3 AFTER MOVE-IN

- Unpack essentials first (kitchen, bathroom, sleeping area)

- Check again for:
  - slow leaks
  - drainage issues
  - anything missed during walkthrough

- Submit any **new maintenance requests clearly**:
  - what the issue is
  - where it is
  - when you noticed it

## DAY 4–5

- Break down boxes and remove trash properly

- Confirm:
  - internet is working
  - all utilities are running correctly

- Test:
  - heating or cooling system
  - hot water consistency

## DAY 6–7

- Do a final check of the unit:

  Look for:
  - anything still not working
  - anything getting worse

- Make sure:
  - no unauthorized changes were made
  - unit is clean and organized

- Save:
  - your photos
  - your condition checklist
  - any emails or maintenance requests

## SIMPLE RULES TO FOLLOW DURING THIS WEEK

- Report problems early, not later
- Do not attempt repairs yourself
- Keep everything documented
- Use the unit normally, but pay attention to how it functions

## BOTTOM LINE

This first week sets your baseline.

- The walkthrough + your notes = your protection
- Early reporting = faster and cleaner fixes
- Clear records = fewer issues at move-out
